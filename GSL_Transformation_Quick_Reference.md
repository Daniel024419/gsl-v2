# GHANA SCHOOL OF LAW
## Transformation Summary Under Act 1170
### Quick Reference for Website Messaging

---

## THE 5 KEY TRANSFORMATIONS

### 1. FROM MONOPOLY PROVIDER → COMPETING PROVIDER

**BEFORE (Pre-Act 1170):**
- GSL: Only institution delivering Law Practice Training in Ghana
- No competition
- Students had to come to GSL for LPTC
- GSL set own standards

**AFTER (Under Act 1170):**
- GSL: One of multiple accredited LPTC providers
- Competes fairly with other universities and institutions
- Students choose among accredited providers
- CLET sets and enforces standards for all
- GSL must meet same standards as others

**WEBSITE MESSAGE:**
"The Ghana School of Law continues to deliver the Law Practice Training Course as one of Ghana's leading accredited providers. Subject to rigorous CLET quality standards and annual inspections, GSL combines traditional excellence with modern legal education."

---

### 2. FROM INDEPENDENT → DIRECTORATE UNDER CLET

**BEFORE (Pre-Act 1170):**
- Ghana School of Law: Autonomous institution
- Own Board of Governors
- Own leadership independent of national structure
- Own regulations and policies
- Set own priorities

**AFTER (Under Act 1170):**
- Ghana School of Law: Directorate of CLET
- Governed by CLET Board
- Principal reports to CLET Director-General
- Follows CLET-wide standards and policies
- Contributes to national legal education mission

**WEBSITE MESSAGE:**
"As a directorate of the Council for Legal Education and Training, the Ghana School of Law operates within Ghana's national legal education regulatory framework, ensuring alignment with international standards and national professional requirements."

---

### 3. FROM SINGLE FOCUS → MULTIPLE SPECIALIZED ROLES

**BEFORE (Pre-Act 1170):**
- Primary mission: Law Practice Training only
- Serve Bachelor of Laws graduates
- One-year intensive professional course

**AFTER (Under Act 1170):**
GSL now operates **FOUR distinct programmes:**

1. **Law Practice Training Course (LPTC)**
   - Bachelor of Laws → One year → Professional qualification
   - One provider among many
   - Subject to CLET standards

2. **Post-Call Law Course** (NEW STATUTORY ROLE)
   - Foreign lawyers → 6-12 months → Ghana practice pathway
   - Sole provider of this programme
   - Unique service to international legal community

3. **National Bar Examination Remedial Courses** (NEW)
   - All candidates preparing for Bar Exam
   - Flexible short-course options
   - Success-focused instruction
   - Competitive with other providers

4. **Specialised Professional Development Programmes** (NEW)
   - Practising lawyers and professionals
   - Emerging legal practice areas
   - Flexible formats and timing
   - Innovation in legal education

**WEBSITE MESSAGE:**
"Today's GSL is a multifaceted legal education institution. Beyond our flagship Law Practice Training Course, GSL serves Ghana's international legal community through our Post-Call Course, helps national bar exam candidates succeed, and offers cutting-edge professional development for practising lawyers."

---

### 4. FROM REGULATING OTHERS → BEING REGULATED

**BEFORE (Pre-Act 1170):**
- GSL: Only LPTC provider, no oversight needed
- GSL: Set standards for law programmes
- No external quality assurance
- Self-regulated institution

**AFTER (Under Act 1170):**
- GSL: Must meet CLET accreditation standards
- CLET: Accredits GSL (like all other providers)
- Annual CLET inspections mandatory
- Subject to quality assurance framework
- Compliance monitoring and reporting
- Can face suspension/revocation if non-compliant
- Right to appeal to Legal Education Appeals Tribunal

**WEBSITE MESSAGE:**
"The Ghana School of Law operates under CLET accreditation and rigorous quality assurance standards. Annual institutional inspections and compliance reviews ensure we maintain excellence in legal education and meet Ghana's and international standards."

**Display on website:**
- CLET Accreditation Status (Accredited - Full/Provisional)
- Accreditation Certificate
- Latest Inspection Report Summary
- Compliance Status

---

### 5. FROM ISOLATED → INTEGRATED IN NATIONAL SYSTEM

**BEFORE (Pre-Act 1170):**
- GSL: Standalone institution
- Limited formal connections to other law faculties
- Independent curriculum development
- Separate from university law programmes
- Minimal coordination with others

**AFTER (Under Act 1170):**
- GSL: Part of integrated national legal education system
- Formal relationship with all university law faculties
- CLET coordinates curriculum standards
- Institutional accreditation framework connects universities to GSL
- Admission pathways clearly defined
- Quality assurance applies system-wide
- Partnerships with courts, government, Bar

"The Ghana School of Law is proud to be Ghana's premier institution in the national legal education system governed by the Council for Legal Education and Training. Through collaboration with universities, the judiciary, law firms, and government, we contribute to developing Ghana's professional legal community."

## NEW PROGRAMMES EXPLAINED

### PROGRAMME 1: LAW PRACTICE TRAINING COURSE (LPTC)

**What It Is:**
- One-year intensive professional course
- Required for any law graduate to practice law
- Combines classroom and practical training
- Leads to Law Practice Training Certificate

**Who Takes It:**
- Bachelor of Laws graduates (from any CLET-accredited university)
- Seeking to become qualified lawyers
- Planning to sit National Bar Examination

**Curriculum (5 Core Areas):**
1. Civil Procedure (including evidence)
2. Criminal Procedure (including evidence)  
3. Advocacy, Ethics & Law Practice
4. Conveyancing & Drafting
5. Law of Evidence

**Practical Components:**
- Law clerk work (with judges/magistrates)
- Law firm internships
- Legal aid clinic (serving needy clients)
- Government agency placements

**Certificate Outcome:**
- Law Practice Training Certificate
- Required to sit National Bar Examination
- Valid in Ghana for legal practice

### PROGRAMME 2: POST-CALL LAW COURSE

**NEW PROGRAMME - UNIQUE TO GSL**

**What It Is:**
- Specialised programme for foreign lawyers
- Teaches Ghana-specific law and practice
- Pathway to Ghana legal practice
- Gateway to enrolment on Roll of Lawyers

**Who Takes It:**
**Category A: Foreign Lawyers**
- Qualified and licensed in analogous legal system
- From country with reciprocal arrangement with Ghana
- Example countries: UK, Nigeria, South Africa, other common law jurisdictions
- Seeking to practice law in Ghana

**Category B: Reciprocal Jurisdiction Citizens**
- Citizens who have met educational requirements in reciprocal jurisdiction
- Who seek to be enrolled on Ghana Roll of Lawyers
- May or may not have been licensed in their home jurisdiction

**Duration:**
- Variable (6-12 months, specific duration TBD by CLET)
- Can be full-time or part-time options

**Content Covers:**
- Ghana's legal system and constitution
- Ghana's civil procedure (High Court and lower courts)
- Ghana's criminal procedure
- Ghana's substantive law (property, contract, criminal, family, commercial)
- Professional conduct standards in Ghana
- Law practice management in Ghana context
- Practical experience with Ghana courts and lawyers

**Why This Is Unique:**
- GSL is the **ONLY institution** offering this programme in Ghana
- Statutory role under Act 1170, Section 78
- Serves international legal market
- Positions GSL as gateway to Ghana's legal profession for foreigners
- High-value programme (international lawyers fees support institutional operations)

**Outcome:**
- Post-Call Law Course Certificate
- Enables application to General Legal Council for Roll of Lawyers enrolment
- License to practice law in Ghana

- Who qualifies (common law jurisdictions)

- FAQs specific to international lawyers

**Marketing Angle:**
"Ghana School of Law welcomes qualified lawyers from around the world. Our Post-Call Law Course is your pathway to joining Ghana's legal profession and serving Ghana's growing international legal market."

### PROGRAMME 3: NATIONAL BAR EXAMINATION REMEDIAL COURSES

**What They Are:**
- Short-term preparation courses for Bar Exam candidates
- Focuses on examination success
- Flexible options and scheduling
- Taught by experienced faculty and practitioners

**Who Takes Them:**
- Candidates preparing for National Bar Examination
- Those needing additional help
- Candidates re-sitting failed subjects
- Anyone wanting targeted, exam-focused instruction

**Programme Options:**

**Option A: Full Intensive Preparation (8-12 weeks)**
- Full-time or flexible scheduling
- Covers all 5 examination subjects comprehensively
- Daily lectures, discussions, practice
- Includes mock full-length examinations
- Cost: Single fee for complete preparation

**Option B: Subject-Specific Remedial (4-6 weeks)**
- Choose one or more subjects needing help
- Flexible scheduling (evenings/weekends)
- Deep-dive into specific examination content
- Practice problems and feedback
- Cost: Per subject

**Option C: Mock Exam & Review (2-3 days)**
- Full-length mock exam under actual exam conditions
- Expert review and feedback
- Performance analysis
- Improvement strategies
- Offered right before actual exam

**Option D: One-on-One Tutoring**
- Private sessions with expert faculty
- Customized to individual needs
- Flexible scheduling
- Cost: Per hour or package

**Examination Content Covered:**
1. Civil Procedure (with evidence component)
2. Criminal Procedure (with evidence component)
3. Advocacy, Ethics & Law Practice
4. Conveyancing & Drafting
5. Law of Evidence

**Success Focus:**
- Teaching to examination format
- Emphasis on frequently tested areas
- Time management strategies
- Practical application of law
- Confidence building

**Website Section Should Include:**
- Clear programme options with pricing
- Flexible scheduling and timing
- Instructor expertise (experienced Bar exam educators)
- Success rates and testimonials
- Sample questions and past papers
- Mock examination schedule
- Learning materials available
- Registration and enrolment process
- Contact and support
- FAQs about exam prep
