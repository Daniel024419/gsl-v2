## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY: GSL'S TRANSFORMATION

### THE FUNDAMENTAL SHIFT

**BEFORE (Ghana School of Law):**
- Sole provider of professional legal education in Ghana
- Delivered Law Practice Training (Professional Law Course)
- Published own regulations and standards
- Operated independently with own governance

**AFTER (Ghana School of Law as CLET Directorate):**
- One operational arm of CLET (not the sole provider)
- Continues Law Practice Training but alongside other accredited institutions
- Operates under CLET Board governance and standards
- Delivers additional programmes beyond traditional LPTC
- Must comply with CLET quality assurance and accreditation standards
- New mission: serve specific constituencies under CLET coordination

## PART 3: GSL'S CORE PROGRAMMES & COURSES

### PROGRAMME 1: LAW PRACTICE TRAINING COURSE (LPTC)

#### Overview
- **Duration:** One (1) Academic Year
- **Eligibility:** Bachelor of Laws degree holders
- **Status:** One accredited provider among many (previously monopoly provider)
- **Accreditation:** Must meet CLET standards and undergo annual inspection
- **Certificate:** Law Practice Training Certificate upon successful completion

#### Curriculum (Per Act 1170, Section 62)

**Mandatory Content:**

1. **Civil Procedure** (with Evidence Component)
   - Civil procedure rules and practice
   - Civil evidence and proof in civil litigation
   - Practical exercises and case studies
   - Drafting civil pleadings

2. **Criminal Procedure** (with Evidence Component)
   - Criminal procedure rules and practice
   - Criminal evidence and proof in criminal trials
   - Practical exercises and scenarios
   - Understanding witness examination

3. **Advocacy, Ethics & Law Practice**
   - Advocacy techniques and oral argument
   - Professional ethics and responsibilities
   - Law practice management
   - Basic accountancy for law firms
   - Client relations and professional civility
   - Practice conduct standards

4. **Conveyancing & Drafting**
   - Conveyancing procedures
   - Contract drafting
   - Will preparation
   - Deed drafting
   - Corporate documents
   - Real property transactions

5. **Law of Evidence**
   - Rules of evidence in civil and criminal matters
   - Documentary evidence
   - Examination of witnesses
   - Expert evidence
   - Privilege and confidentiality

#### Training Methods

**Classroom Instruction:**
- Lectures on substantive law and procedure
- Case analysis and discussion
- Hypothetical problem-solving
- Group exercises and simulations

**Practical Experience:**
- Ad hoc legal services to needy clients (legal aid clinic)
- Law clerk work (pupillage with judge or magistrate)
- Administrative agency placements
- Law firm engagement and internship
- Client interviews and counselling
- Court appearances and trial observation

**Assessment Methods:**
- Written examinations
- Coursework and assignments
- Practical demonstrations
- Client interviews
- Court appearances
- Supervisor evaluations

#### Learning Outcomes

Upon completion, students should be able to:

- **Knowledge:**
  - Understand civil and criminal procedure comprehensively
  - Know evidence rules and their application
  - Understand law practice management
  - Know professional ethics requirements

- **Skills:**
  - Draft legal documents accurately
  - Present oral arguments effectively
  - Interview clients professionally
  - Prepare cases for trial
  - Manage legal matters competently
  - Conduct legal research
  - Provide legal advice

- **Professional Competence:**
  - Ready to serve clients
  - Meet professional standards
  - Apply law to practical situations
  - Work within ethical boundaries

#### Facilities Required (CLET Standards)

**Physical Infrastructure:**
- Permanent office and classroom facilities
- Law library (physical and electronic)
- Computer laboratory with internet
- Mock courtroom (or access to courts)
- Client interview rooms
- Staff offices
- Student common areas

**Staffing:**
- Principal/Director (law teacher with appropriate qualifications)
- Full-time teaching staff
- Guest practitioners
- Supervisors for placements

**Learning Resources:**
- Law library with national and international reports
- Statutes and constitutional documents
- Case law databases
- Legal journals and articles
- Technology resources
- Contact information for supervising judges, magistrates, law firms

### PROGRAMME 2: POST-CALL LAW COURSE

#### Overview
- **Duration:** Variable (to be determined by CLET, typically 6-12 months)
- **Eligibility:** Foreign lawyers or reciprocal jurisdiction candidates
- **Purpose:** Facilitate entry to Ghana's legal profession
- **Certificate:** Upon successful completion
- **Enrolment Pathway:** Entry to Roll of Lawyers (via General Legal Council)

#### Candidate Categories (Section 78, Act 1170)

**Category A: Foreign Lawyers**
- Qualified and licensed to practice law in country with analogous legal system
- Country has reciprocal arrangement with Ghana
- Example: Common law jurisdictions (UK, Nigeria, South Africa, etc.)
- Seek enrolment on Ghana Roll of Lawyers

**Category B: Reciprocal Jurisdiction Citizens**
- Citizens meeting educational assessments for reciprocal jurisdiction
- Have satisfied requirements for admission in analogous legal system
- May or may not have been admitted in that jurisdiction
- Seek enrolment on Ghana Roll of Lawyers

#### Curriculum & Content

**Mandatory Areas:**

1. **Ghana-Specific Legal System**
   - Ghanaian constitutional law
   - Ghanaian civil procedure
   - Ghanaian criminal procedure
   - Ghana evidence law

2. **Professional Conduct in Ghana**
   - Ghana Bar standards and ethics
   - Professional responsibility under Ghana law
   - Solicitor vs. barrister distinction (if applicable)
   - Law practice regulation in Ghana

3. **Ghanaian Substantive Law**
   - Property and conveyancing
   - Family law (marriage, divorce, succession)
   - Contract law (Ghanaian common law + statutory)
   - Criminal law (Ghanaian codes and statutes)
   - Commercial law (Ghanaian framework)
   - Administrative law

4. **Law Practice in Ghana**
   - Court system and procedures
   - Legal profession structure
   - Law office management
   - Attorney-client relations in Ghana context
   - Practical skills applicable in Ghana

5. **Assessment of Prior Qualifications**
   - Recognition of foreign legal education
   - Credit for equivalent courses already taken
   - Targeted instruction for gaps in Ghana-specific knowledge

#### Teaching Methods

**Classroom Components:**
- Lectures on Ghanaian legal system
- Case analysis using Ghana cases
- Statute interpretation
- Group discussions
- Comparative law instruction

**Practical Components:**
- Visits to Ghana courts
- Observation of court proceedings
- Meeting with Ghana Bar leaders
- Mentorship with Ghana lawyers
- Supervised legal work
- Client interviews in Ghana context

**Assessment:**
- Examinations on Ghanaian law
- Practical demonstrations
- Assignments
- Supervisor evaluations

#### Learning Outcomes

Graduates should be able to:
- Understand Ghana's legal system and civil/criminal procedure
- Apply Ghana law correctly
- Understand professional conduct requirements in Ghana
- Serve clients competently in Ghana
- Conduct legal practice according to Ghana standards
- Recognize differences from their home jurisdiction

#### Admission Pathway

1. **Complete Post-Call Course** at GSL вњ“
2. **Pass Examination** (if required)
3. **Receive Post-Call Certificate** from GSL
4. **Apply to General Legal Council** for Roll of Lawyers enrolment
5. **Register as Lawyer** in Ghana

### PROGRAMME 3: REMEDIAL COURSES FOR NATIONAL BAR EXAMINATION

#### Overview
- **Purpose:** Support candidates preparing for or re-sitting National Bar Exam
- **Flexibility:** Intensive short courses or semester-long options
- **Timing:** Offered before examination dates
- **Teaching:** By experienced GSL faculty and Bar practitioners

#### Course Options

**Option A: Full Bar Exam Preparation (Intensive)**
- **Duration:** 8-12 weeks intensive
- **Schedule:** Full-time, evenings, or weekend options
- **Coverage:** All five examination subjects comprehensively
- **Format:** Lectures, discussions, practice questions, mock exams
- **Cost:** Single fee for complete preparation

**Option B: Subject-Specific Remedial**
- **Duration:** 4-6 weeks per subject
- **Enrol in:** One or more subjects as needed
- **Content:** Deep dive into specific examination areas
- **Format:** Focused instruction and practice
- **Ideal for:** Candidates who need help with particular subjects

**Option C: Mock Examination & Review**
- **Duration:** 2-3 days before actual examination
- **Content:** Full-length mock exam under actual exam conditions
- **Analysis:** Post-exam review with expert instructors
- **Feedback:** Detailed performance analysis and improvement strategies

**Option D: One-on-One Tutoring**
- **Duration:** Flexible, by appointment
- **Content:** Customized to individual candidate needs
- **Format:** Private sessions with experienced faculty
- **Cost:** Per-session or package basis

#### Examination Content Covered (Section 71, Act 1170)

1. **Civil Procedure** (including Civil Evidence component)
2. **Criminal Procedure** (including Criminal Evidence component)
3. **Advocacy, Ethics & Practice of Law**
   - Law practice management
   - Basic accountancy
   - Professional responsibility
4. **Conveyancing & Drafting**
5. **Law of Evidence**

#### Teaching Approach

**Instructional Methods:**
- Expert faculty from GSL and practicing lawyers
- Examination-focused curriculum
- Emphasis on frequently tested areas
- Practical application of concepts
- Time management strategies for examination

**Learning Materials:**
- Study guides and outlines
- Past examination questions
- Model answers
- Practice problems
- Textbooks and references
- Online resources and modules

**Assessment:**
- Practice questions and exercises
- Topic quizzes
- Mock full-length examinations
- Performance feedback and improvement plans

#### Candidate Support

**Services Provided:**
- Study schedule guidance
- Examination registration assistance
- Venue and logistics information
- Stress management and test-taking strategies
- One-on-one consultation
- Post-exam support for re-sit candidates

## PART 6: TRANSFORMATION HIGHLIGHTS - WHAT'S NEW

### KEY CHANGES UNDER ACT 1170

This section should appear prominently on the GSL website under "About GSL" в†’ "What's New":

#### What GSL Used to Do

- **Monopoly Provider**: GSL was Ghana's only institution delivering professional legal education (Law Practice Training)
- **Independent Regulation**: GSL set its own standards and regulations
- **Standalone Institution**: Operated independently with own governance structure
- **Limited Programmes**: Primarily focused on Law Practice Training for Bachelor of Laws graduates

#### What GSL Does Now

**1. Continues Law Practice Training - But Not Alone**
- GSL remains an LPTC provider
- Now one of several accredited institutions (universities and private training providers)
- Must compete fairly with other accredited LPTC providers
- Subject to CLET quality assurance standards
- Undergoes annual CLET inspections

**2. New Role: Post-Call Law Course Provider**
- Serves foreign lawyers from analogous legal systems
- Serves citizens from reciprocal jurisdiction countries
- Provides Ghana-specific legal education pathway
- Gateway to Roll of Lawyers enrolment
- GSL is the sole provider of this programme (statutory role)

**3. National Bar Examination Remedial Courses**
- Offers preparation and remedial courses for examination candidates
- Helps students succeed in national qualification exam
- Flexible course options and scheduling
- Expert faculty and targeted instruction

**4. Specialised & Professional Development Programmes**
- Designs and delivers programmes on emerging legal practice areas
- Serves practicing lawyers seeking professional development
- Contemporary topics (technology law, sustainability, etc.)
- Flexible formats (intensive, part-time, online potentially)
- Innovation in legal pedagogy and methods

**5. Subject to CLET Governance**
- Director reports to CLET Director-General
- Board governance through CLET Board
- Must comply with CLET accreditation standards
- Subject to annual quality assurance inspections
- Part of national legal education regulation system

#### Strategic Advantages Under New System

вњ… **Specialisation**: Focus on unique areas (post-call course, international lawyers)
вњ… **Quality Standards**: Benefit from CLET-wide quality framework
вњ… **Collaboration**: Network with other accredited institutions
вњ… **Innovation**: Freedom to develop specialised programmes
вњ… **Professional Development**: New role serving practising lawyers
вњ… **International Recognition**: Aligned with CLET's international standards

#### How This Benefits Students & Stakeholders

**For LPTC Students:**
- Assurance of CLET quality standards
- Fair competition ensuring affordability
- Access to multiple provider options
- Professional recognition of GSL certification
- Opportunity to choose best-fit provider

**For Foreign Lawyers:**
- Clear pathway to Ghana practice via Post-Call Course
- Ghana-specific legal education
- Networking with Ghana legal community
- Entry to Roll of Lawyers assistance
- Only institution offering this programme

**For Bar Exam Candidates:**
- Flexible remedial course options
- Expert exam preparation
- Intensive or part-time options
- Success-focused instruction
- Competitive pricing

**For Government & Courts:**
- Assurance of trained legal professionals through CLET standards
- National quality control of legal education
- Clear professional pathways
- International standards compliance
